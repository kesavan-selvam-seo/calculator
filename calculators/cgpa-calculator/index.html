CGPA Calculator - Calculate CGPA, SGPA and Percentage Online

The CGPA Calculator helps students calculate Cumulative Grade Point Average using semester SGPA, subject grade points, credits and academic performance data. You can use it to calculate CGPA from semesters, SGPA from subjects, CGPA to percentage and target CGPA planning.

This tool is useful for school, college and university students who follow a 10-point grading system. It gives quick results with total credits, total credit points, estimated percentage and academic standing.

What is CGPA?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average. It shows the overall academic performance of a student across multiple semesters or academic terms.

Unlike SGPA, which represents one semester, CGPA combines performance across completed semesters. It is commonly used in colleges, universities and academic records.

What is SGPA?

SGPA stands for Semester Grade Point Average. It measures academic performance in one semester. SGPA is usually calculated using subject credits and grade points earned in that semester.

CGPA is often calculated by combining the SGPA of different semesters with their respective credit weight.

CGPA Formula

The common credit-weighted CGPA formula is:

CGPA = Σ (SGPA × Semester Credits) / Σ Semester Credits

This method gives more importance to semesters with higher credit load. It is more accurate than simply averaging all SGPAs when semester credits are different.

SGPA Formula

The SGPA formula uses subject credits and grade points:

SGPA = Σ (Subject Credits × Grade Point) / Σ Subject Credits

For example, if a subject has 4 credits and you score 9 grade points, then the credit points for that subject are 4 × 9 = 36.

CGPA to Percentage Formula

CGPA to percentage conversion depends on the institution. Some schools and boards use CGPA × 9.5, while some universities use CGPA × 10 or their own formula.

S.No Conversion Method Formula Common Use
1 CBSE / Common Method Percentage = CGPA × 9.5 Often used for CBSE-style conversion
2 Direct 10x Method Percentage = CGPA × 10 Used by some colleges and universities
3 Custom University Rule As per official institution formula Best for official academic use
Important: Always use your university or board’s official CGPA to percentage formula for admissions, jobs, transcripts or official documents.

Example CGPA Calculation

Suppose a student has completed three semesters with the following SGPA and credits:

S.No Semester SGPA Credits Credit Points
1 Semester 1 8.2 20 164
2 Semester 2 8.6 22 189.2
3 Semester 3 8.9 21 186.9

Total credit points are 540.1 and total credits are 63. So the CGPA is:

CGPA = 540.1 / 63 = 8.57

CGPA vs SGPA

CGPA and SGPA are related, but they are not the same. SGPA is calculated for one semester, while CGPA is calculated across multiple semesters.

S.No Term Full Form Meaning
1 SGPA Semester Grade Point Average Performance in one semester
2 CGPA Cumulative Grade Point Average Overall performance across semesters

How Target CGPA Planning Works

The target CGPA planner calculates the average SGPA you need in future credits to reach a desired CGPA.

Required Future SGPA = [(Target CGPA × Total Future Credits) - Current Credit Points] / Future Credits

This helps students understand whether their target CGPA is realistic based on remaining semesters and credits.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a CGPA Calculator?

A CGPA Calculator is an online tool that calculates Cumulative Grade Point Average using semester SGPA, credits, subject grade points or total credit points.

How do I calculate CGPA?

To calculate CGPA, multiply each semester SGPA by its credits, add all credit points, and divide by total credits.

What is the formula for CGPA?

The common formula is CGPA = Σ(SGPA × Semester Credits) / Σ Semester Credits.

What is the formula for SGPA?

The SGPA formula is SGPA = Σ(Subject Credits × Grade Point) / Σ Subject Credits.

How do I convert CGPA to percentage?

Common methods include Percentage = CGPA × 9.5 or Percentage = CGPA × 10. Use your institution’s official formula for accurate academic use.

What is the difference between CGPA and SGPA?

SGPA shows performance in one semester, while CGPA shows cumulative performance across multiple semesters.

Can I calculate target CGPA?

Yes. The target CGPA planner estimates the SGPA required in future credits to reach your desired CGPA.
