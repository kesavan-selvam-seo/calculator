What is a Dice Roller?

A Dice Roller is a digital tool that simulates the action of rolling physical dice. Instead of using real dice, you can choose the number of dice, select the dice type and click the roll button to get a random result.

Online dice rollers are helpful when physical dice are not available, when you are playing remotely, or when you want a quick random outcome for learning or entertainment.

How Random Are the Dice Results?

This Dice Roller uses browser-based random number generation to create a result within the selected dice range. For example, a D6 gives a result from 1 to 6, while a D20 gives a result from 1 to 20.

Each roll is generated separately. Previous results do not control the next result. This makes the tool useful for casual games, practice and learning.

Dice Probability Basics

With one fair six-sided die, each number from 1 to 6 has the same chance of appearing. The probability of rolling any one number is 1 out of 6, or about 16.67%.

With two six-sided dice, there are 36 possible combinations. Some totals are more common than others. For example, a total of 7 can happen in more ways than a total of 2 or 12.

S.No Dice Event Probability

3 Rolling a specific number on one D20 1 in 20, or 5%
4 Rolling a specific number on one D100 1 in 100, or 1%
