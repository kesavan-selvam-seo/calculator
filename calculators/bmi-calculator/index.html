What is BMI?

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It is a simple value calculated from a person’s height and weight. BMI is commonly used as a screening tool to estimate whether an adult is underweight, in a healthy weight range, overweight or in an obesity range.

BMI does not directly measure body fat. It should be used as a starting point, not as a complete medical diagnosis.

BMI Formula

The standard BMI formula for metric units is:

BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)2

The BMI formula for US units is:

BMI = [weight (lb) / height (in)2] × 703

Example BMI Calculation

Suppose a person weighs 70 kg and has a height of 175 cm. First, convert height into metres: 175 cm = 1.75 m.

BMI = 70 / (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.9

A BMI of 22.9 falls within the healthy weight range for most adults.

What is a Healthy Weight Range?

The healthy weight range shows the approximate weight range that may place your BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 for your height. This is useful because two people with different heights will have different healthy weight ranges.

For example, a taller person may have a higher healthy weight range than a shorter person. That is why this calculator shows your weight range based on your entered height.

What is BMI Prime?

BMI Prime compares your BMI with the upper limit of the healthy BMI range. It is calculated by dividing your BMI by 25.

BMI Prime = BMI / 25

A BMI Prime below 1 usually means the BMI is below the upper healthy limit. A BMI Prime above 1 means the BMI is above the upper healthy limit.

What is Ponderal Index?

Ponderal Index is another body measurement value that compares body weight with height cubed. It is sometimes used along with BMI to understand body build.

Ponderal Index = weight (kg) / height (m)3

Unlike BMI, which uses height squared, Ponderal Index uses height cubed. This can make it useful when comparing people at very short or very tall height ranges.

BMI for Children and Teenagers

This calculator is mainly designed for adults. For children and teenagers aged 2 to 19 years, BMI is usually interpreted using age and gender-based percentile charts.

Risks of High BMI

A high BMI may be linked with higher risk of health problems, especially when combined with other risk factors. These may include high blood pressure, type 2 diabetes, heart disease, sleep-related breathing problems, joint stress and other metabolic concerns.

However, BMI alone cannot confirm a health condition. A healthcare provider may also consider waist size, blood pressure, blood sugar, cholesterol, activity level and medical history.

Risks of Low BMI

A very low BMI may suggest that a person is underweight. Being underweight can sometimes be linked with poor nutrition, low energy, weakness, lower immunity, vitamin deficiency or other health concerns.

If your BMI is very low or your weight has changed quickly without a clear reason, it is better to speak with a qualified healthcare provider.

Limitations of BMI

BMI is useful, but it has limitations. It does not measure body fat directly. It also does not separate muscle, fat, bone and water weight.

For example, athletes and people with high muscle mass may have a high BMI even when their body fat is not high. Older adults may have different body composition even with the same BMI as younger adults.

For a better health assessment, BMI should be considered along with other factors such as waist measurement, physical activity, food habits, sleep, blood pressure, cholesterol and medical advice.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a BMI Calculator?

A BMI Calculator is an online tool that calculates Body Mass Index using your height and weight. It helps adults understand their weight category quickly.

How do I calculate BMI?

BMI is calculated by dividing weight in kilograms by height in metres squared. The formula is BMI = weight (kg) / height (m)².

What is a healthy BMI range?

For most adults, a BMI from 18.5 to less than 25 is considered a healthy weight range.

What does BMI Prime mean?

BMI Prime compares your BMI with 25, which is the upper end of the healthy BMI range for most adults. It is calculated as BMI divided by 25.

What is Ponderal Index?

Ponderal Index compares weight with height cubed. It is another measurement that may help understand body build along with BMI.

Is BMI accurate for athletes?

BMI may not be fully accurate for athletes because it does not separate muscle from fat. A muscular person may have a high BMI without having high body fat.
